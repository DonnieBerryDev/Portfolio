The importance of networking within the creative industries

source: Cambridge Dictionary

Networking is absolutely essential in the creative industries. Especially if you are a freelancer. If you have any hope to be successful in the industry, people must know of either you yourself or your previous work.

So, how do I network?

With modern social media, it is the easiest it has ever been to successfully network. I have personally found success in LinkedIn networking. Although, there is different methods and it is up to you to find which is more successful for yourself or in which you feel more comfortable. I will list some very common methods with a brief description below.

LinkedIn

Online social media platform used for business pages and employees to communicate and post status updates or available job roles. You are able to connect with other users which means you can chat to them and see some more information about their profession. In-turn, they could also see yours, which could lead to some potential freelance opportunities for example.
You can visit the website here.

Meetups

In web development, this is a huge networking tool. Meeting other developers and potential future work with employers can gain you some clients from the meeting alone. It is always important to look as professional as possible. This may mean a professional dress code, mannerisms and overall portraying yourself in a very positive light. Now is time to sell yourself as a business. One resource to use is the website Meetup.com.
You can visit the website here.

What skills do I need?

To network, not much skill is actually needed. You just need to make sure that you have people skills.

When chatting with a potential client/employer, it is absolutely vital to maintain a professional standpoint and sell yourself. This means providing evidence of previous work and discussing your skillset. You absolutely must be prepared when attending meetups or speaking with one of these people. Somebody who is not prepared comes across as lazy and not trustable if they were to hire you for your services.

You must always remain positive and try your best to answer all of your potential client/employer’s questions the best you can. One tip I can give is if you do not know the answer to their question, answer it with something positive such as “I don’t know, but I could certainly find out for you!”

There is thousands of resources out there on the internet for you to use to learn how to network. Simply watching YouTube videos on the topic gives a massive amount of information and you can teach yourself how to network. What do I gain from networking?

Depending on your current employment status - whether you are full-time or a freelancer, your gains may vary. In web development, a freelancer may gain additional clients which in turn could earn him more money, plus a new recommendation from a new client, plus potential new clients which are gained from the initial client’s recommendation.

A full-time web developer may end up with a new role which offers a higher salary and more learning opportunities. If your name is out there, employers will be keeping a close eye on your work so it is always important to keep your portfolio up to date.

According to a survey published by Lou Adler, he states that "85% of All Jobs are Filled Via Networking". Hopefully, this gives you an insight into how crucial it is to network yourself in 2002.

Summary

As mentioned before, networking has never been easier. Use all of the points mentioned in this blog to your advantage and give it a go. It will be a slow start most likely, but over time, you will gain more and more confidence and earn yourself some new clients.
