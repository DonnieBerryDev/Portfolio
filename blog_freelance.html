Is Freelancing the road to success?

With the ever-growing list of jobs in the creative industry rising by the year, the percentage of people who are sole freelancers is also rising. We must ask ourselves the question of:
“Why would people choose freelance over a full-time role with a reputable business?”

Hopefully in this blog, I can answer that question.

There is many advantages and disadvantages to going down the freelance route and these vary from person to person. For some people, a full-time role may be more suitable. I will list below the advantages and disadvantages of going down the freelance route but I will be making references to my industry which is the web development industry, but the points still apply to the creative industry as a whole.

Advantages

Be your own boss

By being self-employed, you will oversee all of your affairs and only you (unless you hire additional staff such as a finance manager)

For a lot of people, this can be an advantage as it gives a sense of consistency and not being tied town to specific timetables, giving you the sense of freedom.

The Pay

Since you will not be working with a large business/small agency, you can retain 100% of the profits from the work you are given by your clients. This is a large reason that most people go freelance. Although, since you will be working solo, you will have to manage your time correctly to make sure you are not taking on too much work at once, which in turn will slow down your entire workflow.

Being freelance is the number one way to make more money in the web development industry as you can make way above the salaried amount for most roles

The Ability to choose what you work on

You will be able to decide for yourself which projects to take on. If something serves no benefit to you either financially or education wise, maybe it is not wise to take on and you are under no obligation to undertake the work. With a full-time role, you may not have much say in the matter.

If you are given a very long and tedious website to build that you won’t gain much from, you can simply decline and maybe recommend a developer who would be better suited.

Disadvantages

Not paid on time

A large problem with freelancing is because you are in charge of your own affairs, it is up to you to discuss with the client payment times. If you are dealing with an agency or a larger business, you may experience extended wait times in getting paid for your work. According to a survey from bill.com, 54% of freelancers in the UK admit that they do not get paid on time.

Filing your own tax

You will have to file your own tax returns instead of it automatically being deducted from a monthly salary. Many freelancers opt to hire a finance manager to take care of this for them.

No full-time benefits

A very large reason that people stick with full-time roles is the benefits. Things such as a guaranteed income at the end of the month, holiday pay, sick-pay, employee benefits etc. Losing these benefits could be catastrophic for some people so you need to make sure that you are willing to lose these benefits and adjust accordingly.

Initially gaining clients

You absolutely must network yourself to be successful as a freelancer. How will anybody know you are available if you don’t have an online presence? Things such as setting up a LinkedIn profile, attending meetups, gaining recommendations from previous clients. These things can help you gain more clients and gain a solid clientele list.

How to be a successful Freelance Web Developer

I have touched on a few topics during this blog that answer this question. Things such as networking yourself and recommending different developers to complete tasks for a client instead of yourself. The main thing you should focus on is gaining a great reputation for yourself.

“How can I gain a reputation for myself?” you may ask. The main key is consistency in your work. Make sure your work is always up to the clients standard and be a great point-of-contact. This means replying to emails swiftly and do not take a long time to respond. Keep in touch with your client, let them know what stage you are at in the work and what the estimated time is remaining in the project if possible.

After you have completed the work for the client and the client is satisfied, it is always important to gain feedback. Get a recommendation from the client and ask them (if appropriate) to tell a colleague or friend about your services. This will gain you clients over time and be known as a reputable freelance developer.
